# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Paper Shell serves everyday and long-form writers who want a lightweight desktop editor that stays out of the way while they think and write. They need a calm writing surface, fast access to document context, and an AI partner that can discuss, inspect, and safely act without taking over authorship.

## Product Purpose

Paper Shell is a compact, informative writing editor for uninterrupted drafting and revision. Its AI panel is a pair-thinking and controlled-execution companion: it helps writers examine assumptions, structure ideas, propose edits, and generate document-grounded artifacts such as mind maps. Success means the writer remains in control and can move from thought to deliberate action without leaving the editor.

## Brand Personality

Restrained, quiet, trustworthy. The product should feel like a dependable writing instrument rather than a conversational destination or an AI spectacle.

## Anti-references

- A ChatGPT clone where the chat interface becomes the product.
- AI-first writing tools that prioritize automatic generation or ghostwriting over the writer's own thinking.
- Generic SaaS dashboards with decorative cards, metrics, and visual noise.
- Agent interfaces that hide actions, mutate documents without confirmation, or overstate what they have completed.

## Design Principles

- Keep the document primary. AI supports the writing surface and never competes with it.
- Make agency visible. Show what the agent intends to do, what context it used, and what actually happened.
- Preserve authorship. Prefer discussion and proposals; apply document changes only after explicit confirmation.
- Earn trust through restraint. Use familiar controls, concise copy, and predictable behavior.
- Keep artifacts grounded. Edits, outlines, and mind maps must trace back to the current document.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA-equivalent interaction quality for the desktop UI: keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, sufficient contrast, non-color-only status communication, and reduced or nonessential motion. Destructive or document-mutating actions require a readable preview and explicit user confirmation.
